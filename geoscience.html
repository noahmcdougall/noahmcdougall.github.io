GEOSCIENCE

Zinc Mining

I'm currently employed by Nyrstar Tennessee Mines as an Exploration Geologist. Our task is to broadly steer the direction of the mine and expand the known ore reserves. Accomplishing this is a complicated chess game of coordinating surface and underground drill rigs, incorporating geophysical surveys, and reading through published works. I've never been in a role where time management was so crucial. Get behind in logging core, and it's checkmate.

Working around giant mining machinery and drill rigs puts me outside my element. I have more experience with mining data than ore, but I've found that is one of my greatest strengths in the team. My background in computing and geographic information systems (GIS) has enabled us to integrate our data and plan entire drilling programs instead of going hole-by-hole.

In the beginning...

It started as a summer job working with my best friend. Entertaining tourists about the history and discovery of Tuckaleechee Caverns always bored me; I was far more fascinated in how the bizzare, underground landscape in my hometown formed. Any time I noticed something new, or if a guest asked me a question I didn't know the answer to, I would spend my evenings researching the topic. This curiosity is what pushed me to explore geoscience as a career.
